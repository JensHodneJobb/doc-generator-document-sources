# Safety

## Risk Assessment and Risk Reduction

A risk assessment is performed on all HMH products with the objective of reducing risk to the lowest practicable level to prevent harm to personnel, property and environment.
- To achieve safety in the design of HMH products, general principles and methodology for risk assessment and risk reduction is based on and performed according to ISO 12100
- The preferred hazard analysis method used is FMEA (Failure Mode and Effect Analysis), with the objective to make our equipment inherently safe by design, as far as reasonably practicable
- Information for safe use is integrated in the user manual, including recommended working practices and warnings of residual risk

## Safety Design
- For safety of machinery- electrical equipment of machines, IEC 60204-1 is applied
- For safety of hydraulic and pneumatic systems ISO 4413 and ISO 4414 is applied
- Emergency stop system is in accordance with ISO 13850 with minimum ISO13849-1 Performance Level d
- Equipment for overhead installation is secured according to Dropped Objects Awareness and Prevention" (DROPS) and amendments by the Norwegian "Working Together for Safety" organization. The best practices of these two organizations are followed.

## Access
- Access ways for operating the equipment are in accordance with ISO 14122-1, which is the main reference
- Access for maintenance and emergency of cranes and other equipment is designed according to EN 13586

## Ergonomics
- Control panels and other control devices for manual operation of equipment are placed in safe locations.
- Ergonomic design of drilling control rooms and crane cabins is according to ISO 11064

## Noise
- CE marked equipment is supplied with information about noise emission in accordance with the Machinery Directive 2006/42/EC.
- Information about noise from non-CE marked equipment can be provided by HMH as an option
- Noise measurements, when required, are typically performed according to ISO 3746 or ISO 9614-2
- Modifications or additions to meet the noise level for a room or area are beyond the scope of this specification.

## Hazardous Area Classification

Equipment is delivered according to the hazardous area classification specified by the customer in each project, and is detailed in the equipment specifications submitted with the bid from HMH.
- Electrical and electronic equipment for hazardous areas is based on IEC 60079, and certified to ATEX or IECEx, as applicable.
- For non-electrical equipment in hazardous area ignition hazard assessment (IHA) is performed according to EN 1127-1 and ISO 80079.  This is part of HMH basic design risk assessment.
- The technical file, including the IHA, is reported according to the ATEX directive and filed with the Notified Body as mandated by legislation or contract.
- All deliveries are certified for gas group IIB and temperature class T3 (≤ 200 °C) or stricter.
