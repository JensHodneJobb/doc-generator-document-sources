# Documentation and Marking of Equipment

## Standards for Documentation
- The documentation is based on the NORSOK Z-018.
- The standard file format for documentation is electronic pdf.
- Coding of documentation is according to the HMH Coding Manual

## Supplier Master Document List (SMDL)

Document deliverables by HMH shall be highlighted in a supplier master document list (SMDL), to be issued by HMH within two weeks after order.

The content reflect HMH' s product standardization, and will depend on product type, with respect to scope and disciplines involved.

Documents that contain relevant interface information are issued for customer's review. Customer-HMH turnaround time on documents shall not exceed 10 working days. Upon exceeding said 10 days HMH may code the documents as appropriate.

Other documents, not relevant for interface, are issued for information only.

## Manufacturing Records
- Manufacturing records (Manufacturing Record Book, MRB) is produced and recorded in parallel with the production of a product designed and delivered by HMH
- The records are used as part of a verification process, by the HMH quality surveyors, or by the classification society, when applicable
- It will contain the manufacturer's compiled records for the components, elements, and qualifications used during the fabrication of specific equipment
- The MRB is filed electronically in the HMH documentation archive system
- After finalizing production, the MRB is retained for a minimum of 10 years
- The MRB will normally not be distributed to customers, except as noted for drilling riser.
- Customers may, upon request and agreement, view the document at HMH or subcontactor's premises.
- A customer data book comprising a summary of MRB can be supplied as an option, for information.

## Documentation for Installation, Operation and Maintenance (IOM)
- HMH will regard the equipment within the scope of this specification as equipment supplies, and the IOM is defined as such, (as opposed to engineering supplies)
- Documentation related to plant engineering, except as necessary for interface, is outside the scope of this specification
- IOM shall contain the documentation necessary to install, operate, maintain, and preserve the equipment in a safe manner
- The NORSOK Z-018 is used as a foundation for the IOM.
- The IOM Manual is a standard text document, and the drawings are contained in a separate package.
- IOM is defined on the supplier master document list (SMDL).
- IOM is issued for information only
- IOM is filed electronically in the HMH archive system, where it is retained for a minimum of 10 years after finalizing the production of the equipment.

## Identification of Delivered Products

### Coding of Equipment Modules (Tag Numbering)

Equipment modules and components are identified in accordance with the ISO/IEC 81346 standard, as necessary for operation and maintenance, as detailed in HMH's code manual.  The identification codes are labelled on the physical deliveries and referred to in the IOM documentation as applicable.

### Facility Tagging

A facility tagging system, which identifies equipment uniquely across the facility, is not part of HMH's standard documentation.

Facility tagging ispart of the plant design, beyond the scope of this specification.

Upon request and agreement HMH may, however, mark the equipment with an additional facility tag number provided by the customer, and add a special front page to the standard IOM document.

Customer's main tag number is noted on the front page, but is not referred to elsewhere in the document.
