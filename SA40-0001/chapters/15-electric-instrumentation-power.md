# Electric Instrumentation and Power Systems

## General
- Electrical installation within the equipment is performed according to IEC 61892-6, and IEC 60204-1 as applicable
- If the above-mentioned electrical installation is located in hazardeous area, IEC 61892-7 and IEC 60079-14 are added.
- Electric equipment, instruments and components located in hazardous area: Ref. Chapter 7.6 Hazardous Area Classification
- Tagging of components and wires inside cabinets and junction boxes according to HMH's SF01 - Coding Manual, that is based IEC 81346-2
- Ingress protection (IP) is according to table 15-1. Degrees of protection are defined in IEC 61892-1 .

**Table 151 - Standard protection degree for drilling equipment**


| Equipment | Minimum protection degree |
|---|---|
| Outdoor installed field equipment | IP56 |
| Outdoor motors, general | IP56 |
| Outdoor motor in Draw work and Top Drive with forced ventilation | IP44 |
| Indoor motors | IP44 |
| Indoor equipment (depending on zone) | IP44 |
| Control cabinet in Local Instrument Room (LIR) | IP23 |
| Cabinets in weather protected field area | IP55 |


## Electrical Cabinets

The electrical cabinets will contain PLC's, servers, remote I/O's, network, power, and field termination. All cabinet assemblies are performed according to internal HMH workmanship manual.
- Each control cabinet can be powered by one of these alternatives:
- AC Power with one incoming feeder
- AC Power with two incoming feeders connected to contactor, or via redundancy power supplies/modules

## Cables

Field cables used in the equipment manufactured by HMH are:
- Flame retardant according to applicable parts of IEC 60332
- Fire resistant when required by rules and function according to applicable parts of IEC 60331
- Resistant to oil and drilling fluids according to NEK TS 606
- Armored or braided, as selected by HMH.
- Low smoke and halogen free

Cables where the above-mentioned specifications can't be met (e.g., flying lead at proximity switches), will have short lengths and be terminate in a nearby junction box. For added protection, these cables will be placed in hydraulic hoses or stainless-steel pipes

HMH is responsible for the cables within the equipment only, unless otherwise detailed in the respective product specification SA04. (Some equipment may for practical reasons be supplied with cable for connection to nearby interface point. Details are given in SA04 if applicable)

## Cable Entry and Gland
- Cable entries and electrical interface points will be clearly indicated on the respective equipment general arrangements (GA)
- Cable glands are arranged on the bottom or on the side walls of the junction box. When required, Multi Cable Transit (MCT) is used.
- Ex e type cable glands are delivered as standard for hazardous areas. Ex d glands are used as required for the application.
- Run-through type cable glands are standard delivery from HMH
- EMC glands are used when applicable according to rules and regulations.
- Glands for customer's cables shall be supplied by customer.

## Junction Box
- The junction box design enables the cable(s) to be taken from the entry point(s) to the connections in such a way that chafing against connections or other parts is obviated. The terminal bar and block assembly are designed to avoid impairment by the insulation shrinkage on the effectiveness of the electrical connections.
- The junction box is made of stainless steel 316 material or GRP, certified as Ex e for hazardous area when applicable. The degree of protection grade is IP 56.
- The junction box will be completely supplied with sufficient earth stud or PE/IE/ISE terminal for connection of cable's sheet, armor or earth conductor to meet the requirements for earthing of the equipment.

## Thermistors

VFD controlled motors will be fitted with thermistors or other temperature detecting devices embedded in the stator windings from vendor. It is a safety device to prevent overheating.

Other motors will be delivered according to requirements and specifications.

## Earth
- The protective earth (PE) will be connected to the ground at both ends using the power cable.

This is normally done with cable armour and through gland. If installation requires armour to be terminated in gland (i.e Exd gland), the cable will be fitted with insulated earth conductor. PE conductor will not be terminated in gland for equipment supplied with voltage above 50V
- Instrumentation earth (IE/ISE) will be floating in the field and earthed in the cabinet
- IE and/or ISE bus bars in junction boxes and cabinets are mounted on isolators.
- Screens for Profibus cables and cables on certain VFD driven equipment will be earthed in both ends
- Skids will be equipped with minimum two earth bosses, located on diagonally opposite corners welded to the skid main structure. Each earth boss is drilled for M10 bolt.

## Motor Heating

All outdoor motors with a rated power of 15 kW or above are fitted with internal heater elements in the windings.
