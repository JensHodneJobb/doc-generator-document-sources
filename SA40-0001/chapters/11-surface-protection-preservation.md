# Surface Protection and Preservation

## Paint System
- Equipment structures and main components HMHexposed to marine atmosphere are painted according to HMH standards, conforming to ISO 12944-9 class CX. (corresponding to Norsok system 1B)
- HMHVendor components may be coated according to vendor's standard based on ISO 12944-5 or equivalent, at the discretion of HMH.
- Drilling riser elements for immersed service are painted to ISO 12944-9 class Im4 (corresponding to Norsok system 7.)
- Isocyanate based coatings are not used by HMH.
- The internals of hydraulic oil tanks and dry bulk tanks are considered non-corrosive during operation. A coat of primer is applied for temporary protection before use.
- Stainless steel surfaces are normally not painted, except insulated pipe and vessels, some architectural items, and hydraulic p-lines in drilling riser system. These items are painted with 2 coats epoxy system.
- Parts of the mud and cement system that are exposed to erosion may be supplied unpainted and designed with adequate allowance for corrosion and erosion

## Galvanizing
- Equipment suitable for dipping may be hot dip galvanized according to ISO1461, as deemed practical by HMH
- Typical outfitting structures like handrails, platforms, and bolted derrick members can be hot dip galvanized
- Unless specifically requested and compensated by the customer, galvanized surfaces are not painted.
- The following items are normally not hot dip galvanized due to risk of damage:
- Elements with critical dimension tolerances
- Elements of high strength steel or complex nodes with thick material
- Riser elements

## Preservation

Preservation is defined as temporary protection prior to commissioning and initial operation.

The preservation of equipment is based on the principles of NORSOK Standard Z-006, as relevant for supplier's phase, which includes:
- Establish procedure for initial preservation, as applicable to scope of supply.
- Perform initial preservation before delivery to customer.
- Prepare instructions or checklists for maintenance preservation to be continued by customer or others after delivery from manufacturer.
- Issue the preservation records, checklists and labels to document the preservation status as supplied by HMH, and to enable continuation by others.
- The preservation is performed according to standardized procedures, developed for overseas transport and storage at construction site for a total period of 12 months after delivery. Longer preservation time shall be mutually agreed between HMH and the customer
- The preservation records and checklists for the customer actions after delivery, are given in the Installation, Operation and Maintenance, documentation. Copies shall also be enclosed with the shipment, as part of the dispatch dossier.
